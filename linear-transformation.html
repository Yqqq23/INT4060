Linear Transformation

Watch space transform through matrices

Adjust the matrix values to see how space transforms. Watch the grid deform!

\[A = \begin{bmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{bmatrix}\]

Observe: What's Happening?

As you change the matrix values, the entire grid transforms. Every point in space moves according to the transformation rule defined by the matrix.

\[\begin{bmatrix} x' \\ y' \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} x \\ y \end{bmatrix}\]

The transformed position is the matrix multiplied by the original position

Notice how the basis vectors (Ã® and Äµ) move to new positions - these are actually the columns of your matrix!

Understand: Properties

  • Determinant: Measures how area changes. Negative = reflection, Zero = squishes to line
  • Lines stay lines: Straight lines remain straight (that's what makes it "linear")
  • Origin stays fixed: The point (0,0) never moves in a linear transformation
  • Grid stays parallel: Parallel lines remain parallel after transformation
    • Key Insight: A matrix doesn't just transform vectors - it transforms the entire space!

    Apply: Common Transformations

  • Rotation: \(\begin{bmatrix} \cos\theta & -\sin\theta \\ \sin\theta & \cos\theta \end{bmatrix}\) - Spins space around origin
  • Scaling: \(\begin{bmatrix} s_x & 0 \\ 0 & s_y \end{bmatrix}\) - Stretches or compresses axes
  • Shear: \(\begin{bmatrix} 1 & k \\ 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix}\) - Slants space like pushing a deck of cards
  • Reflection: \(\begin{bmatrix} -1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix}\) - Flips across an axis
