Observe: What's Happening?

As you drag the red dot, you're creating a target vector. Notice how the purple and green lines scale to reach your target? These scaled versions of the basis vectors always combine to form your target vector.

\[\vec{v} = a\vec{i} + b\vec{j}\]

Any vector v can be written as a linear combination of basis vectors

Understand: What Are Basis Vectors?

Basis vectors are like the "building blocks" of a vector space. Just as you can describe any location in a city using street addresses, you can describe any vector using basis vectors.

  • Span: Together, basis vectors can create any vector in the space
  • Independence: No basis vector can be made from the others
  • Coordinates: The coefficients (a, b) are the vector's coordinates in this basis
